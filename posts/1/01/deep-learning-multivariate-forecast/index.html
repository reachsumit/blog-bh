Deep Learning for Multivariate Time Series Forecasting

Statistics vs Machine Learning

Traditionally, statistical methods have been widely adopted for time series forecasting applications. Various empirical studies have shown that the statistical methods generally outperform machine learning algorithms on both accuracy and compute requirements, especially when it comes to univariate time series forecasting. One such popular research, done by Makridakis et al [^1 ] in 2018, compared ten machine learning methods against 8 statistical methods on more than 1,000 time series data from M3-Competition. This dataset contained series from a wide range of industries and had a range of different time intervals. Their results found that classical methods like ETS, Theta, ARIMA outperformed machine learning and deep learning methods like CART, KNN, MLP, RNN and LSTM.
